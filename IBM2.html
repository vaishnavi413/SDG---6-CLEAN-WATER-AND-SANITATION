SDG 6 :- Clean Water and Sanitation Data Analysis

This heatmap shows the percentage of the population using safely managed drinking water services from 2016 to 2022, categorized by urbanization
level (National, Rural, Urban). National Level: High and stable percentages, around 87%-89%. Rural Level: Lower percentages, consistently 80%-81%. Urban Level: Highest percentages, stable at 94%-95%. Urban areas have better access to safely managed drinking water compared to rural areas, indicating a disparity in water service management.

Kiribati: Urban areas had much higher access than rural areas in 2018. Cook Islands, Niue, Tonga, Tuvalu: Very high access to improved drinking water sources in 2016.

Explanation for 6.2.1 Population practicing open defecation.
Fiji: Open defecation decreased to 0% by 2020.
Papua New Guinea: High rates, especially in rural areas, around 18% by 2022. Solomon Islands: High rates, with rural areas around 58% by 2021.
Kiribati: Gradual decrease, with rural areas still high at 47.45% by 2022.

Explanation for 6.2.1 Population with basic handwashing facilities on premises.
Fiji: Significant increase in basic handwashing facilities, reaching 86.53% nationally by 2022.
Papua New Guinea: Low but steady increase, with 29.86% nationally by 2022.
Solomon Islands: Data available until 2019, with 38.64% nationally.
Vanuatu: Major improvement, reaching 75.88% nationally by 2022.

Explanation for 6.2.1 Population using safely managed sanitation services.
Fiji: Rural areas have higher access to safely managed sanitation services, around 57% by 2022.
Papua New Guinea: Urban areas have low and stable access, around 28% by 2022.
Kiribati: Gradual increase in access, with rural areas reaching 25.05% by 2022.
Samoa: Declining access, with national levels dropping to 42.93% by 2022.
